Olympic Phryge

Bounjour, my name is Olympic Phryge, based on the traditional small Phrygian hats. I am a symbol of freedom and represent allegorical figures of the French republic.

Inclusivity and Sustainability

The mascots are made from recycled materials, reflecting the Paris 2024 Olympics' commitment to environmental responsibility. Additionally, the Paralympic mascot has a visible prosthetic leg, highlighting the importance of representation and celebrating athletes with disabilities.

Historical Significance

Originating from ancient Phrygia, a region in modern-day Turkey, the cap was adopted by freed slaves in ancient Rome as a mark of their emancipation. During the French Revolution, it became an emblem of the revolutionaries and was worn as a symbol of resistance against oppression. The cap is often depicted in artwork and on historical monuments, such as the famous statue of Marianne, which represents the French Republic.
